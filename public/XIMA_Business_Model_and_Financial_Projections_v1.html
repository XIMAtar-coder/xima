SECTION 1 — Purpose and Mission

Core Purpose: XIMA exists to amplify human potential and help build conscious, accountable communities capable of addressing the challenges of the future.

XIMA is a human potential platform, not solely a hiring tool. The philosophy:

  • Talent discovery beyond credentials — replacing CV-first screening with behavioral and cognitive signal detection via a validated 5-pillar psychometric model
  • Self-awareness for individuals — enabling candidates to understand their cognitive strengths, communication patterns, and growth areas through anonymized XIMAtar profiles (12 archetypes)
  • Responsible team building — giving organizations behavioral intelligence to build diverse, high-performing teams without credential bias
  • Development pathways — connecting people with vetted mentors and personalized development plans aligned to their pillar profile
  • Community infrastructure — building a network effect where talent signals compound across hiring, development, and mentorship interactions

SECTION 4 — Possible Business Models

Model A — B2B Hiring Platform

  • How it works: Companies subscribe for access to the candidate pool and challenge pipeline. Optional success fee on hires.
  • Pricing logic: Monthly subscription (€199–€1,500/mo by tier) + optional success fee (€500–€2,000 per confirmed hire)
  • Target: SMBs and mid-market companies (10–500 employees) hiring 5–50 roles/year
  • Scalability: High — recurring revenue, marginal cost per additional company is near-zero (infrastructure + AI tokens only)
  • Status: Live — current primary model with 3-tier pricing (Starter / Growth / Enterprise)

Model B — Talent Intelligence SaaS

  • How it works: Companies use XIMA for internal workforce behavioral mapping, team composition analysis, and succession planning
  • Pricing logic: Per-seat licensing (€8–€25/employee/month) or annual enterprise contract
  • Target: HR departments in mid-market and enterprise (500+ employees)
  • Scalability: Very high — no hiring dependency, continuous engagement
  • Status: Designed — requires internal assessment mode and team dashboard (roadmap H2 2026)

Model C — Mentor Marketplace

  • How it works: Mentors set rates for coaching sessions and development programs. XIMA matches candidates to mentors using pillar profiles. Platform takes commission.
  • Pricing logic: Mentor-set pricing (est. €50–€200/session). Platform commission: 15–25%
  • Target: Career changers, junior professionals, candidates post-assessment seeking growth
  • Scalability: Medium — supply-constrained by mentor onboarding; marketplace dynamics require critical mass
  • Status: Partially live — mentor portal operational, booking system live, payment not yet integrated

Model D — Candidate Premium

  • How it works: Free candidates get basic XIMAtar + pillar scores. Premium unlocks detailed capability reports, CV-vs-assessment comparison, priority mentor matching, career strategy insights.
  • Pricing logic: Freemium → Premium at €9.90/month or €89/year
  • Target: Active job seekers and career-development-focused professionals
  • Scalability: High — zero marginal cost for digital reports; conversion rates expected at 3–8% of free users
  • Status: Designed — premium tier structure defined, entitlement system live, Stripe not yet integrated

Model E — Enterprise / White-Label Licensing

  • How it works: Large organizations or HR consultancies license the XIMA assessment engine for internal deployment under their brand
  • Pricing logic: Annual license €15,000–€80,000 depending on seat count and customization
  • Target: Large corporates (1,000+ employees), HR consulting firms, universities
  • Scalability: Medium — high revenue per deal but long sales cycles and customization overhead
  • Status: Hypothetical — requires API abstraction layer and multi-tenant isolation (roadmap 2027+)

SECTION 13 — Strategic Vision

Long-term vision: XIMA becomes a global human capability infrastructure — a persistent behavioral identity layer that connects hiring, development, mentorship, and organizational intelligence.

Phase 1 (Now – Year 2): Foundation

  • Establish product-market fit with Italian SMBs (target: 10–35 paying companies)
  • Validate psychometric model with N≥300 assessments and external review
  • Integrate payment processing (Stripe)
  • Publish 3+ case studies demonstrating hiring quality improvement
  • Build mentor supply to 20+ active professionals

Phase 2 (Year 2–3): Scale

  • Expand to DACH and Iberian markets leveraging trilingual infrastructure
  • Launch Candidate Premium tier with Stripe billing
  • Activate mentor marketplace with payment flows and commission structure
  • Reach 80 companies and 12,000 candidates
  • Begin IRT calibration and publish validation white paper

Phase 3 (Year 3–5): Platform

  • Launch Talent Intelligence SaaS for internal workforce analysis (Model B)
  • Offer API access and white-label licensing for enterprise and edu (Model E)
  • Build behavioral talent graph as proprietary data asset
  • Target 250+ companies and 50,000+ candidates
  • Achieve €1.9M+ ARR with 45% EBITDA margin

Phase 4 (Year 5+): Infrastructure

  • XIMA as portable behavioral credential accepted across employers
  • University and vocational training integrations
  • Government workforce development partnerships
  • Behavioral data marketplace (anonymized, GDPR-compliant aggregate insights)
