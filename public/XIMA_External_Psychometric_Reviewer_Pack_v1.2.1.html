1. Executive Overview

XIMA is a professional assessment platform that maps individuals to one of 12 archetypal profiles ("XIMAtars") across five cognitive-behavioral pillars: Computational Power, Communication, Knowledge, Creativity, and Drive.

The assessment consists of 92 items (84 multiple-choice + 8 open-response) distributed across 4 professional fields (Science & Tech, Business & Leadership, Arts & Creative, Service & Operations). Each field contains 21 MC questions following a deterministic cyclic pillar mapping and 2 open-response questions evaluated by a rubric-based scoring engine with optional AI augmentation.

XIMAtar assignment uses Euclidean distance in 5-dimensional pillar space to match respondents to the closest of 12 canonical profiles. The system operates as a decision-support tool under GDPR Article 22 — it does not make autonomous decisions about individuals.

Current validation status: synthetic stability validation (N=5000), construct separation confirmed via PCA, social desirability mitigation via Cognitive Tension methodology. Predictive validity, IRT calibration, and DIF analysis are explicitly NOT yet claimed and require real-world data collection.

5. Validity Claims & Non-Claims

5.1 What We Claim (with current evidence)

✓ Construct separation: PCA on N=5000 synthetic profiles produces 5 separable components. PC1 explains 32.4% of variance (well below 55% risk threshold for unidimensionality).
✓ Cross-pillar independence: Maximum inter-pillar correlation r=0.34 (CP–Knowledge). All pairs below r=0.55.
✓ Archetype separability: All 12 canonical profiles meet >20-point separation and <15% confusion threshold.
✓ Social desirability mitigation: Cognitive Tension methodology eliminates gradient options. No option is universally "best."
✓ Drive construct specificity: Measures Growth Velocity (learning under friction), not static motivation or 'grit' rhetoric.
✓ Content integrity: Runtime hash-lock (djb2) prevents unauthorized item modification. 1-character change triggers fatal FREEZE VIOLATION.

5.2 What We Explicitly Do NOT Claim

✗ Predictive validity: Not yet established. Requires longitudinal criterion data (job performance, tenure, satisfaction). Minimum N≥500.
✗ IRT calibration: No Item Response Theory analysis. Requires N≥300 per item for 2PL model.
✗ Test-retest reliability: No stability data. Requires repeat assessments at 2–4 week intervals, N≥200.
✗ Differential Item Functioning (DIF): No cross-group fairness analysis. Requires N≥200 per demographic subgroup.
✗ Convergent/discriminant validity: No comparison with established instruments (Big Five, CPI, etc.).
✗ Clinical diagnostic: XIMA is NOT a psychological assessment tool. It is a professional decision-support system.

6. Fairness & Bias Considerations

6.1 Social Desirability Mitigation

All MC questions use the Cognitive Tension design principle: each option represents a legitimate professional strategy involving a genuine trade-off. There are no "obviously correct" or "obviously wrong" answers. Options are not arranged on a gradient from poor to excellent. This approach reduces the ability to game responses by identifying the "socially desirable" option.

6.2 Language/Translation Risk

⚠ The assessment is delivered in 3 languages (EN, IT, ES). Translation equivalence has NOT been formally validated through:
  • Back-translation protocols
  • Bilingual sample comparison
  • Measurement invariance testing (multi-group CFA)
This is a known limitation. Pillar score distributions by locale should be monitored via the telemetry layer for systematic differences that could indicate translation bias.

10. Interpretation Guide for HR/Coach

10.1 How to Interpret Pillar Profiles

Each respondent receives a 5-dimensional score profile (0–100 per pillar). The profile represents behavioral tendencies in professional contexts, not fixed personality traits. Key interpretation principles:

  • Relative strengths matter more than absolute scores. A score of 65 in Creativity paired with 40 in Knowledge reveals a specific development profile.
  • The "weakness-first" lens: XIMA is designed to identify the two weakest pillars with the highest growth leverage. Focus coaching conversations there.
  • XIMAtar archetype provides a memorable shorthand for the profile shape, but the underlying pillar vector contains more information than the archetype label alone.
  • Context matters: Scores reflect behavioral preferences in the selected professional field (Science/Tech, Business, Creative, Service). A person may score differently across fields.

10.2 What NOT to Do

⚠ Do not:
  • Use XIMAtar results as a standalone hiring filter or rejection criterion
  • Treat scores as fixed or permanent — they reflect current behavioral patterns, not innate capacity
  • Over-interpret small score differences (e.g., 62 vs. 65 on the same pillar)
  • Compare scores across different professional fields as if they measure the same construct
  • Use results for clinical, medical, or legal determinations
  • Share individual results with third parties without explicit consent

10.3 Recommended Usage in a Hiring Workflow

  1. Pre-screening signal: Use pillar profiles to identify candidates whose behavioral tendencies align with role requirements. Always combine with other evidence (CV, interview, references).
  2. Structured interview preparation: Use the two weakest pillars to design targeted behavioral interview questions that probe growth potential.
  3. Team composition: Map existing team members' profiles to identify capability gaps. Use XIMA data to inform (not determine) new hire priorities.
  4. Onboarding & development: Share results with the candidate as a development tool. Focus on the "growth edge" (weakest pillars) for coaching plans.
  5. Decision-support, not decision-making: XIMA output is one data point among many. Final hiring decisions must always involve human judgment.
