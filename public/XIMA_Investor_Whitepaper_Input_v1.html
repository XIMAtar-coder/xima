1 — Executive Overview

  • What XIMA is: A psychometric hiring intelligence platform that replaces CV-first screening with a 5-pillar behavioral assessment. Candidates complete a structured evaluation and receive an anonymized behavioral profile (XIMAtar) before any company sees their identity.
  • Problem solved: Traditional hiring relies on credential bias (university name, employer brand), unstructured interviews, and subjective gut-feel. These methods produce high false-positive rates, penalize non-traditional candidates, and provide zero behavioral signal to employers.
  • Core innovation: XIMA combines validated psychometric item design with AI-scored qualitative evaluation. The system assigns one of 12 canonical XIMAtar archetypes (e.g., Fox = strategic adaptability, Owl = analytical depth) via Euclidean distance matching on the pillar vector. Companies evaluate behavioral fit before seeing CVs.
  • Scalability: The pillar model is field-agnostic — currently deployed across 4 professional fields (Business & Management, Technology & Engineering, Creative & Design, Social & Education). Trilingual (EN/IT/ES). Archetypes are industry-neutral; field-specific item calibration happens at the question level, not the model level.

2.2 Assessment Pipeline

  • Input: Candidate selects professional field → completes 21 multiple-choice items (4-point ordinal scale, 0–3) + 2 open-ended responses.
  • Pillar mapping: Items follow a cyclic 5-pillar assignment (q1→P1, q2→P2, … q6→P1, etc.). Each pillar receives exactly 4–5 items per field. Mapping is content-locked with djb2 freeze hashes.
  • Scoring: MC scores → pillar means → normalized 0–100. Open answers scored by AI (rubric: depth, specificity, self-awareness, actionability; 1–5 scale) → blended 90% MC / 10% open.
  • XIMAtar assignment: Final pillar vector compared to 12 canonical archetype profiles via Euclidean distance. Nearest archetype assigned. Top-3 matches stored.
  • Output: Candidate profile with pillar scores, XIMAtar identity, strengths/friction summary, development plan items.

3.2 Governance Framework

  • Audit logging: Every AI call logged to ai_invocation_log table with: request_id, correlation_id, prompt_hash (SHA-256), model version, latency, status, scoring_schema_version.
  • Deterministic replay: ai-invocation-replay edge function can re-execute any historical AI call for audit or debugging.
  • Pre-guardrails: Trilingual non-answer detection regex (catches placeholder, offensive, or empty responses). Minimum content-length thresholds.
  • Post-guardrails: Red-flag detection in AI output → score capping. Schema validation (Zod) on all AI responses. Local heuristic fallback if AI call fails.
  • No autonomous decisions: AI outputs are advisory signals. All hiring decisions require human confirmation. System classified as decision-support under GDPR Art. 22.

4.2 Validation Metrics (Synthetic, N=500)

  • Cronbach's α per pillar: 0.60–0.68 (acceptable for 4–5 item scales)
  • Max cross-pillar correlation: r = 0.34 (discriminant validity confirmed)
  • PCA first component variance: PC1 = 32.4% (no single dominant factor)
  • Status: synthetic validation only. Real-world calibration requires N ≥ 300 per field.

4.3 Scoring Architecture

  • MC items: 4-point ordinal (0–3), no reverse-scoring in v1.2
  • Pillar score = mean of assigned items → normalized to 0–100
  • Open-answer: AI rubric scoring (1–5) on depth, specificity, self-awareness, actionability
  • Final blend: 90% MC + 10% open-answer contribution
  • XIMAtar: Euclidean distance to 12 canonical profiles → nearest match assigned

4.4 Freeze Protocol

  • All assessment items content-locked with djb2 hashes
  • Runtime freeze guard validates hashes on assessment load
  • Any item change triggers version increment and mandatory revalidation
  • Current version: v1.2 (frozen 2025-02)

5.2 XIMA Structural Differentiation

  • Candidate-centric model: Unlike all competitors, XIMA gives candidates a persistent, portable behavioral identity (XIMAtar) that is reusable across companies. Competitors treat candidates as single-use inputs to employer-specific pipelines.
  • Progressive disclosure: The 3-level challenge system (L1→L2→L3) provides increasing depth without front-loading assessment fatigue. Competitors use single-stage evaluation.
  • SMB-accessible pricing: Entry at €199/mo vs. enterprise-only contracts ($50k+/yr) for HireVue/Pymetrics. XIMA targets the underserved SMB segment where 80%+ of hiring decisions happen without any psychometric tooling.
  • AI as advisor, not judge: XIMA explicitly positions AI as decision-support (GDPR Art. 22 compliant by design). HireVue has faced regulatory and public scrutiny for opaque AI-driven candidate rejection.
  • Open scoring model: Candidates see their full pillar breakdown and XIMAtar rationale. Competitors keep scoring proprietary.

6 — GDPR & Compliance

  • Data subject rights (implemented): Full data export (JSON), account deletion with cascade, profiling opt-out toggle, consent versioning with timestamped records.
  • Legal basis: Legitimate interest for assessment (with opt-out); explicit consent for AI processing and data sharing with businesses.
  • Article 22 compliance: XIMA is classified as a decision-support tool. No automated hiring decisions. All AI outputs are advisory. Human confirmation required at every pipeline stage.

  • Data residency: Supabase EU region. No data transfer outside EEA except AI inference (Gemini API — covered by Google DPA).

7.2 Key Risks (Honest Assessment)

  • Validation gap: Psychometric model has synthetic validation only. Predictive validity requires real hiring outcome data (N≥300).
  • Single AI provider: All 9 touchpoints use Gemini via Lovable Gateway. Provider lock-in and pricing risk.
  • Market adoption: Behavioral-first hiring is unfamiliar to most SMB HR teams. Requires education-heavy sales cycle.
  • No payment infrastructure: Stripe not yet integrated. Cannot self-serve onboard paying customers today.
